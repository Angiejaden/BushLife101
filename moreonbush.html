Bush Gee at a Glance.

Alliance Girls High School, formerly known as African Girls High School, is a national girls' boarding school located near Kikuyu,just 20km from Nairobi. It was established in 1948 to serve as the first instituition of higher secondary education for African girls in Kenya. Starting with only 10 girls, the school has now grown to have a population of almost 2000 students with a reputation for academic excellece in Kenya. The school featured as the #1 school in the KCSE 2016 results. Currently, the school is popularly known as Bush Gee to distinguish it from its brother school, Alliance High School which is Bush Bee. To house the almost 2000 students, there are 15 dorms popularly known as houses. The most modern one being fondly called, Post Modern which has 6 of the houses. These houses are named after the founding missionaries and notable principals. The exception being Kenya house which is named after the country since it was built in the year the country gained independence. Some notable alumni from this prestigious school at the heart of Kenya include, the late Lucy Kibaki, Sally Kosgei, Charity Ngilu and Betty Gikonyo among others.
